About

This two-year program prepares students with the necessary clinical and administrative knowledge to become entry-level medical assistants.


Students will receive hands-on training and learn how to properly administer injections, take vital signs, record EKGs, implement basic accounting procedures, and understand the fundamentals of patient documentation.


West-MEC programs prepare students to seek one or more industry certification or licensure in their field of study, providing a pathway to economic independence.

CTSO

The Medical Assistance CTSO practices front office simulations, which includes phone calls, scheduling, rooming patients, and small procedures.


The West-MEC Medical Assisting CTSO recently attended the State Leadership Conference on April 25th, bringing Chapters 39376 and 39375, and their advisor, Ms. Michaels.


The seniors of the CTSO recently finished their HOSA Career Display Presentations on March 9th, by gathering all the information on their future career choice and creating beautiful boards and insightful presentations.
